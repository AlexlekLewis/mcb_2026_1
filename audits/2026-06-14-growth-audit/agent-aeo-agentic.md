## Review-schema legitimacy verdict

**Verdict: the underlying reviews are REAL, but the way they're surfaced is a Google review-snippet POLICY VIOLATION (P0). Schema is sourced legitimately; it is just not displayed.**

Evidence chain:

1. **Source is genuine, not fabricated.** The 6 schema `Review` objects + `AggregateRating` are drawn from `src/lib/customer-reviews.ts` (`CURATED_REVIEWS`, `REVIEW_AGGREGATE = {count:47, rating:5.0}`), whose header documents them as transcribed from MCB's real Google Business Profile (`https://maps.app.goo.gl/zRBNX1LBoTc2DK2g9`). Authors (Mohammad Shah, Esther Yew, Vineel Davuluri, Jane McAninly, Jessica Barry, Arafat Mohamed…) read as authentic GBP reviewers. **No evidence of fabrication.**

2. **But the schema reviews are NOT displayed on the page.** Live homepage raw HTML contains the review authors/bodies **only inside `<script type="application/ld+json">`** (and the escaped RSC copy of it). The visible "Feedback from Recent Customers" section is an **Elfsight third-party iframe** (`elfsight-app-7befc592-9c56-4e59-8c2d-5b7073681391`, "Loading Google reviews…") that fetches reviews client-side from Elfsight — a *different* render path than the hardcoded `CURATED_REVIEWS`. So the user sees Elfsight's feed; Google sees 6 hardcoded `Review` nodes that don't appear as on-page text. **Google's policy: review markup must be for content "directly available to users on your page."** This mismatch is precisely what triggers "Reviews not displayed" manual actions / silent rich-result suppression.

3. **Amplified across the whole site.** Because `OrganizationSchema` is mounted in the global layout (`src/app/layout.tsx:83`), the *same* `AggregateRating 5.0/47` + 6 reviews are emitted on all 33k URLs, including every suburb page — the schema-spam pattern CLAUDE.md explicitly forbids. The per-suburb `LocalBusinessSchema` correctly omits the rating (good, and well-commented at `RichSchema.tsx:201-203`), but the layout-level Organization block re-introduces it everywhere, defeating that care.

4. **Where review schema IS clean:** the homepage and product FAQ accordions are genuinely displayed (the FAQ questions/answers appear as visible `<details>` DOM text, 1:1 with their `FAQPage` JSON-LD). FAQPage markup is legitimate. The problem is isolated to the `Review`/`AggregateRating` block.

**Required action (one of):** (a) render the 6 curated reviews as visible HTML (author + stars + body) on the homepage so schema == displayed content, and scope the block to the homepage only; or (b) remove `review` + `aggregateRating` from the Organization schema entirely and let the GBP `sameAs` carry social proof. Do **not** continue emitting review schema that no user sees, on every URL.

## Details — other P0/P1s

**P0 robots vs llms.txt.** The wildcard `User-Agent: *  Allow: /` means GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot/Google-Extended/CCBot are all permitted to crawl (good for GEO). The defect is the *promise*: llms.txt's "Specific bot rules in /robots.txt. Honour those." asserts targeted directives that don't exist. Either ship them or delete the sentence — a verifiably false statement in an AI-facing manifest erodes the exact trust llms.txt exists to build.

**P1 BreadcrumbList dead code.** `Breadcrumbs.tsx` already generates a correct `BreadcrumbList` from `usePathname()`. It is simply never imported/rendered. Mounting it in the category/product/guide layouts is near-zero-risk and immediately adds path-context schema to thousands of deep URLs — high leverage for "X near me" and product-tree disambiguation in AI answers.

**P1 Product/price schema gap.** This is the single biggest *citability* miss. MCB's strategy (per memory) is to publish indicative pricing with protective T&Cs specifically to win AI-search / PAA price answers — but with no `Product`/`Offer`/`priceSpecification` markup, engines must scrape prose and will hedge or omit. Structured `offers` with `minPrice`/`maxPrice`/`priceCurrency:"AUD"` + a T&C `description` (baseline spec, on-site measure required, refresh date) converts the prose ranges into directly-citable facts.

**P1 InlineAnswer under-deployment.** The component is spec-perfect for GEO (BLUF answer, visible-DOM-matches-schema, freshness `Updated <month>`, deep-link anchors Perplexity uses). Shipping it on one page wastes the build. Wiring it into `ProductTemplate` + the guide template, seeded from existing FAQ arrays, is the highest-ROI content move available without writing net-new copy.

## What's already good

- **llms.txt + llms-full.txt exist, are live (200), and are well-structured** — quick-fact block, product taxonomy, suburb list, explicit citation guidance, Q&A capsules. Genuinely above-average agentic hygiene for an SMB.
- **FAQPage schema is legitimate where it ships** — homepage FAQBlock and ProductTemplate FAQ render the Q&A as visible `<details>` DOM text 1:1 with the JSON-LD. No FAQ schema spam.
- **The "don't repeat aggregateRating per suburb" rule is understood and partly implemented** — `LocalBusinessSchema` deliberately omits the rating with a clear code comment (`RichSchema.tsx:201-203`). (The miss is the *layout-level* Org block re-adding it — fixable in one place.)
- **Host/redirect discipline:** apex cleanly 307→www; sitemap, canonicals and og:url all use the **www** host consistently; product & guide pages set self-canonicals.
- **Org schema is rich and accurate:** founders (Deane, Dee), foundingDate, openingHours, areaServed, paymentAccepted, serviceType, priceRange, telephone, email — strong entity core. NAP (1300 732 319, admin@…, Melbourne/Preston VIC) is consistent across llms.txt, schema and `site.ts`.
- **Suburb×product pages emit `Service` + `areaServed(City)`** — correct shape for a service-area business.
- **301 redirect of thin `/products/*` → richer category pages** (`src/app/products/[slug]/page.tsx:52-54`) shows good consolidation instinct.

## Top 3 AEO priorities

1. **Fix the review-schema legitimacy (P0, do first).** Either display the 6 curated reviews as visible HTML on the homepage (so schema == on-page content) **or** remove `review`/`aggregateRating` from the Organization schema — and in the same change, scope that block to the homepage only so it stops appearing identically on all 693 suburb pages + 33k URLs. This removes a manual-action risk and the schema-spam signal in one move. *(Low effort, high risk-reduction.)*

2. **Ship structured price + breadcrumbs to make money pages citable (P1).** Add `Product`/`Service` `offers` with `minPrice`/`maxPrice` + `priceCurrency:"AUD"` + T&C `description` to product/guide pages (turns prose ranges into facts AI engines will quote), and mount the already-built `<Breadcrumbs>` so every deep URL carries `BreadcrumbList`. *(Directly targets MCB's stated AI-price-capture strategy.)*

3. **Scale the answer-first layer + correct the llms/robots claim (P1/P0-trust).** Roll `InlineAnswer` (BLUF answer + freshness date + anchors) into `ProductTemplate` and the guide template across all money pages; add `Article` schema to the 7 guides; and reconcile llms.txt with reality — add a pricing-with-T&Cs fact block, switch the `.txt` host to `www`, and either add the named AI-bot rules to robots.txt or drop the false "specific bot rules" sentence.
