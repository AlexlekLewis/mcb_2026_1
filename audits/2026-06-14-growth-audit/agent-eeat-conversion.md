## Details — P0 / P1

### P0-1 — Surface Deane & Dee as named, experienced owners
The business's single biggest organic trust advantage is that the owners are real, named people who appear *by name* in their own Google reviews ("Amazing service from Dee and Deanne", "Deane and Dee were so professional" — see `customer-reviews.ts`). Yet `/our-story` and `/about` never name them. A visitor cannot connect the warm, personal post-submit email signed "Dee" to anyone on the public site. This is pure asserted-not-demonstrated E-E-A-T. The photo exists (`/assets/mcb-owners.jpg`) — it just needs names, a short first-person bio, years each has been doing this, and ideally what each person does on a job. This is the cheapest high-impact trust fix available and directly reinforces the "family-owned, not a call centre" message that already runs through the copy (`CROSections.tsx:147-152`).

### P0-2 — Make genuine reviews actually visible (don't depend on Elfsight)
Every public reviews surface (home `GoogleReviewsWidget`, all product pages) relies on a single Elfsight third-party script that lazy-loads and shows "Loading Google reviews…" — confirmed still loading on live `/blinds/roller-blinds`. If that script is slow, blocked, or fails, the site has **zero visible social proof** on its highest-traffic pages, despite 12 real 5-star reviews sitting in the codebase. The fix is low-effort because the pattern already exists: `QuoteForm`'s `TrustCard` server-renders `REVIEW_AGGREGATE` (5.0 / 47) plus a curated quote. Reuse that on the home proof bar and product pages so a rating and at least one real review are guaranteed in the initial HTML. Keep Elfsight as a live-feed enhancement underneath. (Note: this stays within the "only MCB / no third-party customer reviews" voice rule — these are MCB's own Google reviews, sourced from the GBP, which the memory explicitly permits in schema/email; rendering them on-page is consistent.)

### P0-3 — Close the pricing-transparency gap
There's a fully-written `/pricing-policy` page promising indicative ranges "as at [Month YYYY]" with baseline specs and price-change drivers — exactly the protective T&Cs the pricing-transparency memory requires. But no product page renders any price, and `ProductTemplate` has no price field. Result: the policy protects prices that don't exist, the AI-search/PAA price-capture opportunity is forfeited, and a savvy visitor who reads the policy then finds no prices may distrust the rest. Decide one way: ship the indicative ranges (with the date stamp + T&Cs the policy already specifies) or trim the policy to match reality. Given the documented AI-search rationale, shipping ranges is the higher-value path — but it's Med effort because it needs a price field in the product model + per-page values.

### P1 — Lead-path friction (the most direct conversion lever at this volume)
The current required set to submit is: suburb, ≥1 product, first name, phone, valid email, **best contact time, project stage, referral source** (+ referrer name if "Friend"/"Repeat customer"). The last three are required and gate the submit button (`section3Valid`, `QuoteForm.tsx:130-137`). windowCount was already correctly relaxed to optional (good, documented at `:83-94`). At 4 leads/month, the priority is removing every non-essential required field: `projectStage` and `referral` are nice-to-have triage the team can ask on the callback. Recommend: required = suburb + product + name + phone + email only; everything in Section 3 optional. This is low-effort (flip the `useMemo` validity gates) and the single most defensible CRO change here.

### P1 — Abuse protection on `/api/quote`
The endpoint writes to Supabase, sends an internal email **and** a customer confirmation email, and fires an optimization run — all per POST, with no rate limit, CAPTCHA, or honeypot. For a site receiving ~4 real leads/month, a trivial spam script could both run up email/DB cost and bury real leads. A hidden honeypot field + min-submit-time check in `QuoteForm` plus a per-IP rate limit on the route adds zero friction for humans. This overlaps the open P1 in the cost-security audit and should be done regardless of CRO.

## What's already good

- **CTA system is disciplined and consistent.** All quote CTAs funnel through `PrimaryCTA` with tracking; copy ("Book Free Measure & Quote") is clear and benefit-led; phone is tap-prominent in navbar (`tel:` link) and repeated beside every CTA. Single clear primary action throughout.
- **Sticky mobile CTA is well-behaved** — appears after 300px scroll, hidden on `/quote` and dashboard, full-width, not intrusive (`StickyMobileCTA.tsx`).
- **Reassurance at point of conversion is strong.** The quote page sidebar carries "free / no obligation / samples brought to you", a 3-step "What happens next", a featured review, a rating, and a phone fallback. "Takes about 60 seconds" + "We'll call within 24 business hours" set clear expectations (`QuoteForm.tsx:280-301, 483-510`).
- **Post-submit experience is excellent.** Success screen confirms the 24-hour callback and offers a phone CTA; the confirmation email is genuinely warm, signed "Dee", with photo, "what happens next", a recap, real reviews and a phone line — best-in-class for a trade business (`api/quote/route.ts`).
- **Out-of-area handling is humane, not blocking** — soft sky-coloured notice, submission still allowed, server re-classifies suburb (`QuoteForm.tsx:335-345`).
- **ChatWidget is a real asset** — answers pricing/warranty/product questions on-brand (no starting prices, qualify-out tone), then routes to book/call/callback. Hidden on `/quote` to avoid clutter.
- **Positioning copy honours the brief** — "not a call centre", "no scripts, no pressure, no upsell", quality-led language that qualifies out price-only shoppers.
- **Form UX details are thoughtful** — Enter-key submit suppression, section progress markers, suburb datalist autocomplete, proper input modes/autocomplete, accessible labels.

## Top 3 conversion / trust priorities

1. **Make trust visible and human (P0-1 + P0-2 together).** Name Deane & Dee with a real bio + photo on `/our-story` and `/about`, and server-render the 12 real 5-star reviews + "5.0 from 47 Google reviews" on the home page and product pages instead of depending on an Elfsight embed that shows "Loading…". Both are low-effort, reuse assets already in the repo, and attack the core E-E-A-T weakness: trust is *asserted* but never *shown* to a human who doesn't submit the form. Biggest trust lift for least work.

2. **Cut quote-form friction to the essentials (P1).** Make `projectStage`, `referral` (and its referrer-name) optional so the submit button unlocks at suburb + product + name + phone + email. At 4 leads/month, removing three required fields is the most direct, lowest-risk conversion lever on the site. Pair with a hidden honeypot + rate limit so loosening the form doesn't invite spam.

3. **Resolve the pricing-transparency contradiction and de-emphasise the finance banner (P0-3 + P1 banner).** Either ship the indicative ranges the `/pricing-policy` already promises (with its date stamp + protective T&Cs, capturing the AI-search/PAA value) or trim the policy to match reality — don't leave a polished policy guarding prices that don't exist. Concurrently, move the Payright finance banner out of the top-of-every-page slot so each page leads with the quality value prop and a visible rating, not interest-free finance — which currently undercuts the intended middle-tier, qualify-out positioning.
